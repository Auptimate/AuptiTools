Have a draft deal document in hand
For example, a Share Purchase Agreement (SPA) or a SAFE, reflecting the Target Investable Amount you've secured from the target asset (e.g. a company).
Confirm your Target Investable Amount matches what's stated in the deal docs
The Target Investable Amount is the figure in your SPA / SAFE — what the target company actually expects to receive.
Total Investable Amount (after fees, from the calculator below) should match the Target Investable Amount in your deal docs
Common mix-up: the Target Investable Amount in your deal docs is not the same as the gross Allocation Amount you'll enter into the calculator below — Allocation Amount is the larger, pre-fee figure at the SPV level. Gross up the Allocation Amount so the resulting Total Investable Amount matches your deal docs.

Economics Fee Mechanics — Understand the Numbers Above
Understand Total Investable Amount ≠ Allocation Amount
The amount investors actually need to invest is the Total Allocation Amount minus the opportunity fee, portal fee, and banking fees — what's left is the Total Investable Amount that flows into the target asset.
Total Investable Amount = Total Allocation Amount − Opportunity Fee − Portal Fee − Banking Fees

At Exit Carry & Distribution Waterfall
A six-step waterfall runs when the underlying asset exits. Deployment fees (portal, opportunity, banking) are not revisited here — they were already netted out of what investors wired in. This waterfall works off gross cash deposited and gross cash returned.
Understand the carry waterfall
Investors are made whole first — for the total cash they deposited into the vehicle, pre-fees. Only the profit left after that (and after a flat SPV distribution fee) is split, with carry paid to you before the rest goes pro-rata to the preference shareholders. You can earn the opportunity fee and carry regardless of whether you invest your own capital (subject to your own tax status) — investing is entirely optional.
Common mix-up: carry is always paid to you as lead — but whether you also share in the pro-rata remainder depends on whether you've added yourself as one of the investors above (tagged "Lead"), not on your role as deal lead.
